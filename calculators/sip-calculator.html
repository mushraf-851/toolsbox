How does SIP investment work?

SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) lets you invest a fixed amount monthly in a mutual fund. Each month your money buys units at the current NAV. Over time you benefit from rupee cost averaging and the power of compounding.

M = P × [(1+r)ⁿ − 1] / r × (1+r)
Where: P = monthly amount · r = monthly rate (annual% ÷ 12 ÷ 100) · n = months
📅 Rupee cost averaging

Investing the same amount monthly means you automatically buy more units when markets fall and fewer when they rise — reducing average cost per unit.

⏰ Power of compounding

Your returns earn returns. Starting 5 years earlier with the same SIP can more than double your final corpus. Time is the most powerful variable.

📈 Step-up SIP

Increasing your SIP by 10% annually (matching salary hikes) dramatically accelerates wealth creation compared to a flat SIP.

🏦 ELSS Tax benefit

ELSS funds qualify for ₹1.5L deduction under Section 80C with a 3-year lock-in and ~13% historical returns — combining tax saving with wealth creation.

Frequently asked questions

What is SIP and how does it work?
SIP lets you invest a fixed monthly amount in a mutual fund. Each month your money buys units at the prevailing NAV. You benefit from rupee cost averaging and compounding over time.
How much can ₹5,000/month SIP grow in 15 years?
At 12% annual returns: Total invested = ₹9L. Maturity ≈ ₹25L. Wealth gained ≈ ₹16L — nearly 2.8x your investment. Use the calculator above for your exact projection.
What is Step-Up SIP?
Step-Up SIP means increasing your monthly investment by a fixed percentage every year — e.g. starting ₹5,000/month and increasing 10% annually. This aligns with income growth and dramatically increases the final corpus.
What return rate should I use?
Use 10-12% for large-cap/index funds, 12-15% for mid-cap, 14-18% for small-cap — based on historical averages. Use a conservative estimate for long-term planning.
Is SIP better than lumpsum?
SIP is generally better for salaried investors — it removes the need to time the market. Lumpsum can outperform if invested at market lows. This calculator lets you compare both scenarios.
