How is GPA calculated?

GPA is a weighted average. Each course's grade points are multiplied by its credit hours to get "quality points." All quality points are summed and divided by total credit hours.

GPA = Σ(Grade Points × Credits) ÷ Total Credits
CGPA = (Previous Quality Points + Current QP) ÷ Total Credits
Percentage = (GPA ÷ Scale Max) × 100
📚 Credit weighting

A 4-credit course has twice the weight of a 2-credit course. Always enter the correct credit value from your transcript.

🎯 Dean's List

Most universities require 3.5+ on a 4.0 scale for Dean's List recognition. Check your institution's specific requirements.

🇮🇳 10-point CGPA

Many Indian universities use 10-point grading. Multiply by 9.5 for the approximate percentage (standard CBSE formula).

📈 Improving GPA

Retaking poor courses, excelling in high-credit subjects, and consistent performance are the fastest ways to raise CGPA.

Frequently asked questions

How is GPA calculated step by step?
1. Get grade points per course (A=4.0, B=3.0, etc.). 2. Multiply by credit hours → quality points. 3. Add all quality points. 4. Divide by total credit hours. That's your GPA.
What is a good GPA?
On 4.0 scale: 3.5–4.0 = Excellent (Dean's List). 3.0–3.49 = Good. 2.5–2.99 = Average. 2.0–2.49 = Below average. Below 2.0 = Academic probation risk at most institutions.
How do I convert GPA to percentage?
4.0 scale: Percentage ≈ (GPA ÷ 4.0) × 100. India's 10-point CGPA: Percentage = CGPA × 9.5 (CBSE formula). Individual universities may have their own conversion tables.
What is CGPA vs GPA?
GPA = one semester's average. CGPA = cumulative average across all semesters. Enter your previous CGPA and total credits in the optional section to calculate your updated CGPA.
How many credit hours should I enter?
Enter the credit hours listed on your transcript or course syllabus. Lectures are typically 3 credits, labs 1–2 credits, major/full-year courses 4–5 credits. Use 3 if unsure.
