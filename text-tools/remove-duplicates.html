How does this duplicate remover work?

Paste any list — one item per line — and the tool scans every line, keeps the first occurrence, and removes every repeat. It runs entirely in your browser, so nothing is ever sent to a server.

The case-sensitive toggle decides whether "Apple" and "apple" are the same entry. Trim whitespace handles lists with irregular spacing. Sort outputs the unique results in alphabetical order.

Frequently asked questions

How do I remove duplicate lines from a list?
Paste your list into the input box and click Remove Duplicates. The tool keeps the first occurrence of each line and removes all subsequent duplicates automatically.
Is the duplicate check case-sensitive?
By default matching is case-insensitive, so "Apple" and "apple" count as duplicates. Enable the Case-Sensitive option to treat them as different entries.
Can I sort the output alphabetically?
Yes — enable Sort A→Z before clicking Remove Duplicates and the unique lines will be returned in alphabetical order.
Will blank lines be removed?
Enable the Remove Blank Lines option to strip all empty lines from the output. By default blank lines are preserved as part of your list structure.
How many lines can this tool handle?
The tool runs entirely in your browser and can easily handle tens of thousands of lines. There is no server-side limit — performance depends only on your device.
