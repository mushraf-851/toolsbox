How is reading time calculated?

Reading time is calculated by dividing total word count by reading speed in words per minute (wpm). Research shows the average adult reads 200–250 wpm for general content, with a median of 238 wpm.

Reading Time = Word Count ÷ Reading Speed (wpm)
Speaking Time = Word Count ÷ 130 wpm (avg presentation speed)
Example: 1,000 words ÷ 238 wpm ≈ 4 min 12 sec

📰 Blog & articles

Ideal blog posts are 1,500–2,500 words (6–10 min read). Long-form SEO content often targets 2,000+ words. Know your reading time before publishing.

🎤 Speeches & presentations

A 5-minute speech ≈ 650 words at 130 wpm. A 10-minute TEDx talk ≈ 1,300 words. Use speaking time to prepare perfectly.

📚 Books & long-form

An average novel has 80,000 words — about 5.6 hours at 238 wpm. Non-fiction averages 60,000 words. Use this to plan your reading list.

📧 Emails & messaging

The sweet spot for professional emails is 50–125 words (under 30 seconds). Longer emails see lower response rates.

Frequently asked questions

What is the average reading speed?
The average adult reads 200–250 words per minute for general content. Research using eye-tracking puts the median at 238 wpm. This tool defaults to 238 wpm but you can set any custom speed.
How long does it take to read 1,000 words?
At average speed (238 wpm), 1,000 words takes about 4 minutes 12 seconds. At slow speed (150 wpm) it's 6 minutes 40 seconds. At fast (400 wpm) it's 2 minutes 30 seconds.
How many words is a 5-minute read?
At 238 wpm, a 5-minute read is approximately 1,190 words. Paste a 5-minute target into the tool to verify your article length.
What is speaking time vs reading time?
Speaking time uses 130 wpm — the average comfortable pace for presentations and speeches. Silent reading is faster at 238 wpm. Use speaking time to prepare talks, podcasts, and voiceovers.
How is word count calculated?
Words are counted by splitting text on whitespace and punctuation. Multiple spaces, newlines, and punctuation-only tokens are excluded. This matches the method used by Microsoft Word and Google Docs.
